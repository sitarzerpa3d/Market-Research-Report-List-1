<p><p>The collagen fiber suture market is divided into two main types: monofilament and multifilament sutures. Monofilament sutures consist of a single strand, offering reduced tissue trauma and lower rates of infection due to their smooth surface. In contrast, multifilament sutures comprise multiple intertwined strands, providing greater strength and flexibility, which facilitates knot security. Both types serve distinct applications in surgical settings, with choices depending on tissue type, healing requirements, and surgeon preference, thus catering to diverse medical needs.</p></p>
