<p><p>The blood cancer therapeutics market is segmented into various types based on leukemia classifications. Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML) involves prolonged proliferation of myeloid cells, often treated with targeted therapies. Acute Lymphocytic Leukemia (ALL) primarily affects lymphoid cells, requiring intensive treatment regimens. Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL) features slow accumulation of leukemic cells, with therapies focusing on managing symptoms and progression. Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) is marked by rapid cell growth and often necessitates aggressive chemotherapy or innovative therapies for effective management.</p></p>
