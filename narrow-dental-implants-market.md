<p><p>The narrow dental implants market primarily includes two types: root form and plate form dental implants. Root form dental implants mimic the natural tooth root, featuring a cylindrical design that allows for integration with the jawbone, making them ideal for areas with adequate bone density. Conversely, plate form dental implants are flat and designed for patients with minimal bone width, providing stability in narrow spaces where traditional implants may not fit. Both types cater to diverse patient needs in dental restoration.</p></p>
