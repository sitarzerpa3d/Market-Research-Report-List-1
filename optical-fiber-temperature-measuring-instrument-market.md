<p><p>The optical fiber temperature measuring instrument market encompasses two primary types: contact and non-contact systems. Contact temperature sensors utilize optical fibers that come into direct contact with the measured object, providing precise temperature readings through thermal conduction. In contrast, non-contact systems rely on the emission of infrared radiation, allowing temperature measurement from a distance without physical interaction. Each type serves specific applications, with contact types ideal for close-range measurements and non-contact types suitable for high-temperature or hazardous environments.</p></p>
